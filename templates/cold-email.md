# Cold email template (canonical pattern, not copy-paste)

The `copywriter` agent uses this as a reference. Every actual email must adapt these structures to the specific lead.

---

## Step 1 — Day 0

**Subject** (≤ 4 words, lowercase, no punctuation):
- `quick roofing question`
- `{{city}} roof referral`
- `re: {{company}}`

**Body** (50–80 words):

```
hi {{first_name}} —

{{personalization}}

we book qualified roofing appointments for residential contractors like {{company}} — 20 per month, owners only, confirmed in writing. paid per appointment or flat retainer.

worth a 12-min call this week to see if the math works for you?

— {{sender_first_name}}
```

---

## Step 2 — Day 3

**Subject**: (reply to Step 1, no new subject)

**Body** (30–50 words):

```
{{first_name}} —

quick context: {{case_study_one_liner}}.

would the same math work for {{company}}? happy to share the numbers on a 12-min call.

— {{sender_first_name}}
```

---

## Step 3 — Day 7

**Subject**: (reply to thread)

**Body** (20–30 words):

```
{{first_name}} —

closing the loop — fair to assume the timing isn't right? happy to circle back in a few months if that's better.

— {{sender_first_name}}
```

---

## Forbidden phrases (instant delete)

- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Just circling back"
- "Quick question" (it never is)
- "Touching base"
- "Synergy", "leverage" (verb), "innovative", "cutting-edge"
- "Let me know your thoughts" (CTA is too vague)
- Anything with an exclamation point in the subject

## Voice notes

- Lowercase opening hi looks like a human, not a marketing template.
- Em-dash and "—" instead of "Best regards / Sincerely" — feels personal.
- One CTA per email, soft ask.
- No images, no logos, no email signature graphic. Plain text gets delivered.
- Sentence per line on the longest body, double-spaced.
