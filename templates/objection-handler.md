# Objection handler — what to say when they push back

Cross-referenced by `reply-triage` (for written objections) and used live by the human on discovery calls. Each entry: the objection in their words, what they REALLY mean, the response that converts, the response that loses.

---

## "How much does it cost?" (asked before discovery)

**They mean**: I want to qualify out fast so I don't waste my time.

✅ "Depends on your volume needs, but pilots are typically $500–$1k and retainers $2k/mo. Worth a 10-min call to see if the math works for your ticket size?"

❌ "$2,000 a month" (no context → instant no)

---

## "That's expensive."

**They mean**: I can't see the ROI yet — show me the math.

✅ "Let's run the math together. What's your average ticket on a residential job? Okay, so ${{ticket}}. And your close rate from a qualified estimate, ballpark? So ${{ticket}} × {{close_rate}}% × 20 appointments = ${{revenue}}. Our fee is ${{price}}. Which part of that math doesn't work?"

❌ "I can drop the price to $1.5k" (you've trained them to negotiate every renewal forever)

---

## "How is this different from [competitor]?"

**They mean**: I'm scared of getting burned again or by hearing the same pitch twice.

✅ "Honest answer — for some businesses they're probably fine. Two things we do differently: (1) we send from our own warmed infrastructure, so your domain reputation never gets touched; (2) we replace any unqualified appointment for free, so you're not paying for tire-kickers. If those don't matter, go with whoever's cheapest."

❌ "We're 10x better than them!" (specifics or it didn't happen)

---

## "I tried [other lead-gen approach] and it didn't work."

**They mean**: Validate my past experience before pitching me something similar.

✅ "What specifically didn't work — were the leads bad, or was follow-up on your side a problem? Because if it's follow-up, we should solve that first; if it's lead quality, that's exactly what we fix differently."

❌ "Oh that was probably them, we're different" (dismissive → trust erodes)

---

## "I'm too busy right now."

**They mean**: Either (a) they really are slammed, or (b) it's a polite no.

✅ "Totally get it. Question — busy because you've got more demand than crew, or because admin is eating you? If it's demand, we should pause and revisit in Q2. If it's admin, that's actually why we'd be a fit — these appointments come to you pre-qualified with confirmed times."

❌ "I'll keep trying you" (you've slammed your own door)

---

## "Send me some info first."

**They mean**: I want to delay the decision.

✅ "Happy to send a one-pager, but it'll just rehash this call. Real question — what specifically do you need before deciding yes or no?"

❌ "Sure, I'll email you a proposal" (you'll never hear from them again)

---

## "Let me think about it / talk to my partner."

**They mean**: Either I want to delay, or there's a real second decision-maker.

✅ Real partner: "Makes sense. Can we get the three of us on for 10 minutes Thursday? I'd rather answer their questions directly than have you relay it."

✅ Delay tell: "I get it. What's the specific question you're trying to answer this week? Maybe I can save you a step."

❌ "Take your time" (you've removed all urgency)

---

## "I don't want to be locked into a contract."

**They mean**: Protect-me-from-a-bad-vendor reflex.

✅ "Fair. That's why the first month is the test. If it sucks, you cancel. If it works, you stay. The 3-month minimum kicks in only at the *start of month 2* — so you get a 30-day exit with a single email."

❌ "We require 6 months" (door closes)

---

## "Your industry is full of scammers."

**They mean**: I've been burned and need proof you're not them.

✅ "Yep, it's real. Two things we do to be the opposite: (1) money-back guarantee — actually money back, not credit; (2) you can talk to my last 3 clients before signing. Want me to make the intros?"

❌ "We're different, trust me" (no proof = no trust)

---

## "I need to see results before I pay."

**They mean**: Reasonable. Sometimes negotiable, sometimes the deal is dead.

✅ Performance model: "I can offer that — $150 per qualified appointment, billed weekly. Lower upside for me, lower risk for you. Fair?"

✅ Money-back: "How about this — pay the $500 pilot up front. If at day 14 you haven't gotten the 5 appointments, full refund. Either I deliver or I eat the cost. Where's the risk?"

❌ "Sure, pay me nothing for the first month!" (they'll just leave at month 1)

---

## "What if I just hire an in-house SDR?"

**They mean**: I want to compare cost of alternatives.

✅ "Smart consideration. SDR salary alone is $4k–6k/mo, plus tools (Apollo, Smartlead) $200/mo, plus 3–6 months to ramp them to your kind of output. We're $2k/mo, day-one ramp, no payroll tax. The math says you'd hire an SDR after $30k+/mo in volume — until then this is cheaper."

❌ "Why would you do that?" (dismissive of their judgment)

---

## "This is a pyramid scheme / MLM / scam."

**They mean**: They're not your buyer. Move on.

✅ "Sorry — definitely not pitching that. Sounds like this isn't a fit. Best of luck."

End the call. Don't argue. Log as `dead-lead`.

---

## Universal closing line (use when stuck)

> "Got it. So, real talk — what would have to be true for this to be a yes today? If it's something I can answer, let's answer it. If it's not, let's not waste each other's time."

This collapses 80% of "maybe" into a real yes or a clean no.
